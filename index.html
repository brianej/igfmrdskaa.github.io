Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) are being described as the state of the art for tasks such as prediction, planning, and interaction, but there is increasing evidence of their critical limitations. In particular, they can generate arbitrary responses in new situations, do not support rapid incremental updates and adaptation based on limited examples, and are opaque. Our work seeks to leverage the complementary strengths of LLMs and knowledge based reasoning systems and to determine the appropriate use of these systems. Specifically, our architecture utilizes LLMs to provide generic abstract predictions of upcoming tasks, reasoning with domain-specific knowledge and semantic databases to jointly implement the current task and prepare for the anticipated task by computing and executing a suitable sequence of concrete actions. Furthermore, the architecture solicits and uses high-level human feedback based on need and availability to incrementally revise the domain-specific knowledge and design contextual queries for subsequent interactions with the LLM.
